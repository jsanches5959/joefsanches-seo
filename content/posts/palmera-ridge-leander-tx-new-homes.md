## What to Watch For as a Buyer

**New community = limited comps.** Because Palmera Ridge is new and still building out, there's limited resale history to validate pricing. When evaluating whether you're paying a fair price, compare against similar new construction in other communities and recent sales in the broader Leander market.

**MUD taxes likely apply.** New master-planned communities in this area almost universally carry MUD (Municipal Utility District) bonds that add to the property tax rate. Ask the builder specifically for the full tax rate — not just the base rate — before you budget.

**Amenities are still coming.** At this stage of development, the full amenity build-out is ahead of you, not behind you. The pool and clubhouse may not be open at the time of your close. That's the trade-off for buying early in a new community — you get the lowest prices but not the finished product.

**Builder incentives are real right now.** Builders in new communities like Palmera Ridge are actively incentivizing to hit absorption targets in 2026. Rate buydowns, design center credits, and closing cost assistance are all on the table. If Palmera Ridge is on your list, negotiate — don't just take the first offer.
