# Best Austin Suburbs 2026: Leander vs Cedar Park vs Round Rock vs Georgetown vs Pflugerville

Austin's suburbs have exploded in the last decade — and in 2026, the differences between them are more meaningful than ever. School districts, property tax rates, commute times, home prices, and community character vary significantly across what looks like a 20-mile radius on a map.

Here's the definitive comparison for buyers trying to choose.

---

| Suburb | Median Home Price | Effective Tax Rate | School District | Metro Rail? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Leander | $430K | 2.1%–2.5% | Leander ISD (#2 metro) | Yes |
| Cedar Park | $400K | 1.9%–2.2% | Leander ISD / RRISD | Lakeline station |

## Leander

**Best for:** Families who prioritize school district quality above all else; new construction buyers; military families using VA loans.

**The case for Leander:** Leander ISD is consistently rated #2 in the Austin metro. Three AP Honor Roll high schools. The new construction market is the most active in the region — Lennar, KB Home, Taylor Morrison, Toll Brothers, and more all have active communities. Metro Rail connects Leander station to downtown Austin, which is a real advantage for two-commuter households.

**The case against:** Property taxes are the highest of any Austin suburb for comparable homes — especially in newer MUD districts. A $450K home in a Leander MUD community can cost $10,500–$11,500/year in taxes. Fast growth means some newer communities are still developing their infrastructure and character.

**Bottom line:** If you have school-age kids and plan to stay 7+ years, Leander's school premium tends to pay off in quality of life and resale demand. If you're childless or planning a short hold, the tax math is harder to justify.

---

## Cedar Park

**Best for:** Buyers who want Leander ISD schools at a lower tax rate; tech workers commuting to the 183A corridor; buyers who value established neighborhoods over brand-new.

**The case for Cedar Park:** Cedar Park is the sleeper pick of the Austin suburbs. A significant portion of Cedar Park falls within Leander ISD boundaries — giving you the same top-rated schools at effective tax rates that are often 0.2%–0.4% lower than Leander MUD addresses. More established neighborhoods mean more mature trees, finished landscaping, and communities that don't have 5 years of construction noise ahead of them.

**The case against:** Less new construction diversity than Leander. Fewer "master-planned community with resort amenity" options. Some buyers find Cedar Park feels less distinct — it blurs into Austin's northwest sprawl.

**Bottom line:** Cedar Park is arguably the best value play in the metro right now. Same schools as Leander, lower taxes, more established feel. The challenge is that the best deals require knowing which specific addresses are in LISD vs. not — you need an agent who can tell you the difference by ZIP code.

---

## Round Rock

**Best for:** Budget-conscious buyers; buyers who value established community infrastructure; Dell/tech workers at Round Rock campuses.

**The case for Round Rock:** Round Rock is the original Austin suburb success story. Round Rock ISD is excellent — A-rated, strong across academics and athletics, with the track record and established programs that newer districts are still building. Home prices are lower than Leander across the board. Downtown Round Rock is more developed than Leander's.

**The case against:** Round Rock ISD vs. Leander ISD: both are strong, but LISD has been edging out RRISD on rankings metrics in recent years. Round Rock is also the most developed suburb, which means less new construction opportunity and more competing inventory on resale.

**Bottom line:** Round Rock is a great choice if RRISD's established programs appeal to you and you want to stretch your budget further. Buyers fixated on LISD rankings should stay north.

---

## Georgetown

**Best for:** Buyers who want lower taxes, Hill Country character, and a walkable historic downtown; buyers at the $350K–$500K range who want more square footage; remote workers.

**The case for Georgetown:** Georgetown's historic downtown square is genuinely special — one of the best in Central Texas. Home prices are competitive, tax rates are among the lowest in the metro for established areas, and the city has invested in quality of life infrastructure. Wolf Ranch is one of the better master-planned communities available at any Austin suburb price point.

**The case against:** Georgetown ISD trails Leander ISD on most metrics. No Metro Rail access. Longer commute to North Austin tech hubs. Less new construction variety than Leander at comparable price points.

**Bottom line:** Georgetown wins on value per dollar and character. It loses on school district and commute. If you work downtown or in North Austin and have kids in high school, the commute and school trade-offs are real. If you're remote or retired, Georgetown is seriously underrated.

---

## Pflugerville

**Best for:** Value buyers; southeast Austin workers; buyers who don't prioritize school district rankings.

**The case for Pflugerville:** Lowest median prices of any major Austin suburb. Close to Tesla Gigafactory (20 min), Samsung Austin Semiconductor, and the southeast Austin tech corridor. Real affordability at the $300K–$400K price point where other suburbs are struggling.

**The case against:** Pflugerville ISD doesn't compete with LISD or RRISD on rankings. Higher effective tax rates than what you'd expect given the home prices. The community character is more industrial/commercial-adjacent than the family-suburb feel of Leander or Georgetown. Commute to North Austin tech hubs is the longest of any suburb on this list.

**Bottom line:** If you work in southeast Austin and prioritize home price over school district, Pflugerville makes sense. Most families with strong school district priorities don't end up here.

---

## The Decision Framework

**If school district is your top priority:** Leander or Cedar Park (both LISD)

**If taxes are your top priority:** Georgetown or Round Rock established areas

**If value per square foot is your top priority:** Pflugerville or Georgetown

**If commute to downtown Austin matters:** Leander (MetroRail option)

**If you want new construction:** Leander has the most selection; Georgetown and Cedar Park have good options

**If you want established community character:** Round Rock, Georgetown, or older Cedar Park
