## VA Loan Basics in Leander (2026 Numbers)

**Loan limit:** The 2026 VA conforming loan limit in Williamson County and Travis County is **$806,500**. If your purchase price is under that — and most Leander homes are — you need zero down payment.

**Funding fee:** If this is your first VA loan use and you're not exempt, the funding fee is 2.15% of the loan amount. This can be **rolled into the loan**, meaning you still don't need cash at closing beyond earnest money. Veterans with a service-connected disability rating of any level are **fully exempt** from the funding fee.

**No PMI — ever.** Conventional loans require private mortgage insurance if you put less than 20% down. VA loans never have PMI, which saves $150–$400/month on a typical Leander purchase.

**Rates:** VA loans consistently price 0.25–0.5% below equivalent conventional loans. On a $450K purchase at current rates, that difference is roughly $80–$100/month — over 30 years, that's real money.

## Texas VLB Loan: The Option Most Texas Veterans Don't Know About

In addition to the federal VA loan, Texas veterans have access to the **Texas Veterans Land Board (VLB) home loan program** — a separate, state-funded option with below-market fixed rates.

**2026 VLB base rate: 6.35% fixed.** Veterans with a service-connected disability rating of 30% or higher qualify for a discounted rate of **5.85% fixed**.

You can **combine** the VLB loan with other programs. The VLB doesn't replace the VA benefit — it's an additional tool Texas provides.

When does VLB make more sense than VA? When the VLB rate is meaningfully lower than VA market rates, and you need a straightforward 15–30 year fixed without the funding fee calculation. Ask me to run the side-by-side comparison for your specific numbers.

## TSAHC Homes for Texas Heroes: Stacking Benefits

Texas State Affordable Housing Corporation (TSAHC) runs a **Homes for Texas Heroes** program specifically for veterans. It offers:
- Below-market mortgage rate
- Up to 5% of the loan amount in down payment assistance (though VA doesn't need this, it can cover closing costs)
- Mortgage Credit Certificate (MCC): up to $2,000/year back on your federal taxes on mortgage interest

If your income qualifies (typically under $120K–$130K in the Austin area), stacking TSAHC with your VA loan can further reduce out-of-pocket costs on a resale purchase.
