# Texas VLB vs VA Loan: Which Is Better for Buying a Home in Leander TX in 2026?

If you're a Texas veteran buying a home in Leander, you have two separate government loan programs available to you — and most real estate agents don't explain the difference clearly. Here's exactly how they compare and which one wins in different scenarios.

## What Is the VA Loan?

The VA loan is a federal benefit available to veterans, active-duty service members, National Guard members, reservists, and eligible surviving spouses. It's the most widely used veteran home loan program in the country.

**Key features in 2026:**
- **Zero down payment** required for loans under the county limit ($806,500 in Williamson County)
- **No monthly PMI** — ever, regardless of LTV
- **Funding fee:** 2.15% of loan amount for first-time use with no disability rating (can be rolled into the loan). **Fully waived** for veterans with any service-connected disability rating.
- **Competitive rates:** VA loans typically price 0.25–0.5% below conventional loans
- Accepted by every major builder and seller in Leander

## What Is the Texas VLB Loan?

The Texas Veterans Land Board (VLB) home loan is a **separate state program** funded by Texas. It offers a fixed interest rate set by the state — separate from federal VA loan market rates.

**2026 VLB rates:**
- **Base rate: 6.35% fixed** (15, 20, 25, or 30-year terms)
- **Disability discount rate: 5.85% fixed** — for veterans with a 30% or greater service-connected disability rating

The VLB loan maximum is $832,750. It requires Texas residency and the home must be your primary residence.

## Can You Use Both?

**Yes — you can combine a VLB loan with VA benefits.** The VLB loan itself is a separate mortgage product; combining it with your VA entitlement essentially means you're getting the VLB's state-subsidized interest rate while the VA entitlement eliminates PMI requirements. A VLB-approved lender can structure this for you.

## Which Is Better? The Actual Comparison for Leander in 2026

**Scenario 1: Veteran with no disability rating**

On a $450,000 Leander home purchase:

- **VA loan:** Market rate ~6.75%, zero down, 2.15% funding fee ($9,675) rolled in. Monthly P&I: ~$3,015
- **VLB loan:** 6.35% fixed, funding fee not applicable. Monthly P&I: ~$2,803

At current market rates, the VLB rate of 6.35% is **meaningfully lower** than what VA loans are pricing in the open market. If you have no disability rating, the VLB loan may save you real money — approximately $200/month on a $450K loan.

**Scenario 2: Veteran with 30%+ disability rating**

- **VA loan:** Funding fee waived (saves $9,675). Market rate ~6.75%. Monthly P&I: ~$3,015
- **VLB disability rate:** 5.85% fixed. Monthly P&I: ~$2,660

The VLB disability rate at 5.85% is significantly below current market. On a $450K loan, that's **$355/month** in payment savings versus standard VA market rates. This is a major benefit that most disability-rated veterans aren't using.

**Scenario 3: Using builder incentives in Leander**

Builders in Leander are offering rate buydowns in 2026. If a builder offers to buy your rate down 1.5 points on a VA loan (bringing market 6.75% down to 5.25%), the VA buydown beats the VLB rate.

**Bottom line:** Compare your specific numbers. The VLB's below-market fixed rate wins on paper in many 2026 scenarios, but builder buydown packages on VA loans can flip that calculation. Run both scenarios before committing.

## How to Get a VA Certificate of Eligibility (COE)

Before you can use either program, confirm your VA eligibility:

1. Visit va.gov → Benefits → Home Loans → Certificate of Eligibility
2. Provide DD-214 (separations), VA Form 26-1880, or allow your lender to pull it directly through the VA's Loan Guaranty system
3. Active duty: Lender pulls it via the automated system with your service records

Most VA-experienced lenders can pull your COE same-day. This is not a bottleneck.

## What to Do Next

If you're a veteran planning to buy in Leander in 2026:

1. Pull your VA COE (or have a lender do it)
2. Get your disability rating confirmed if applicable — it affects both the funding fee waiver and VLB rate eligibility
3. Get pre-approved with a lender who knows both VA and VLB programs — not all lenders originate VLB loans
4. Work with a buyer's agent who understands how VA offers are structured, how VA appraisals work in Leander's builder market, and how to negotiate with sellers who have outdated VA loan concerns
