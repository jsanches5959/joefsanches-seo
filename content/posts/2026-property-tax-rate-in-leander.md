## Understanding Property Taxes in Leander, TX: The Basics

Leander is uniquely positioned, spanning both **Williamson County** and **Travis County**, which affects how property taxes are assessed. In addition, different special districts and the **Leander Independent School District (ISD)** contribute to your overall tax rate.

### Key Property Tax Components in Leander

- **County Taxes**: Differ based on whether your property is in Williamson or Travis County.
- **School District Taxes**: Leander ISD is the primary school district in this area.
- **City Taxes**: Leander city government levy.
- **Special Districts**: Includes MUDs (Municipal Utility Districts) and other districts based on your specific subdivision.
- **Community and Improvement Districts**: Sometimes applicable in master-planned communities like Crystal Falls.

## Frequently Asked Questions About 2026 Property Tax Rate in Leander

### Will property tax rates rise significantly in 2026?

Rates are expected to increase moderately due to inflation and school district funding needs, but no drastic hikes are forecasted—especially in Williamson County subdivisions.

### Can I get exemptions to lower my property taxes?

Yes. Texas offers homestead exemptions, over-65 exemptions, disability exemptions, and military veteran exemptions that can reduce taxable value. I can help you determine your eligibility.

### How often do property tax rates change?

Annually, based on budgets set by counties, cities, school districts, and special districts. The tax rate is applied to the appraised property value assessed by the county.
