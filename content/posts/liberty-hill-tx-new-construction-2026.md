# Liberty Hill TX New Construction 2026: Is It Worth Moving Further Out?

Liberty Hill is 10–15 miles west of Leander and has become one of the most active new construction corridors in Central Texas. Home prices are lower, lot sizes are larger, and you can still get into the suburban Austin market under $350K. The question is whether the trade-offs — longer commute, less established infrastructure — are worth it.

Here's the honest breakdown for 2026 buyers.

---

## What's Driving Liberty Hill Growth

Two things: **price** and **space**. Buyers priced out of Leander or Cedar Park are finding that moving 15 minutes further west gets them an extra bedroom, a larger lot, or $50K–$80K in purchase price savings on a comparable floor plan.

Liberty Hill also sits in **Liberty Hill ISD** — a smaller, rapidly improving district that's investing heavily in new facilities as the population grows. It doesn't have Leander ISD's track record yet, but Liberty Hill High School's rankings have been trending upward.

---

## Active Communities in Liberty Hill (2026)

### Santa Rita Ranch
**Builder:** Multiple builders (Lennar, Perry Homes, others)  
**Price range:** $320K–$550K  
**Schools:** Liberty Hill ISD  
**Notes:** One of the largest and best-amenitized master-planned communities in Liberty Hill. Consistently wins awards for amenity package. Pools, trails, community events. Multiple village sections at different price points. This is the most polished Liberty Hill community.

### Rancho Sienna
**Builder:** Multiple builders  
**Price range:** $340K–$490K  
**Schools:** Liberty Hill ISD  
**Notes:** Hill Country setting with more dramatic topography than flat Leander communities. Feels more rural but with full suburban amenities. Slightly further out than Santa Rita Ranch.

### Stonewall Ranch
**Builder:** D.R. Horton primary  
**Price range:** $280K–$390K  
**Schools:** Liberty Hill ISD  
**Notes:** Entry-level option for Liberty Hill. D.R. Horton's production-build quality is more functional than premium, but the price point is one of the most accessible in the Austin area for new construction.

### Orchard Ridge (Georgetown/Liberty Hill border)
**Builder:** Lennar, others  
**Price range:** $350K–$500K  
**Schools:** Liberty Hill ISD (confirm by address)  
**Notes:** Sits at the border of Georgetown and Liberty Hill; school assignment varies by address. Amenity center with pool, trails.

## Property Taxes in Liberty Hill

Liberty Hill typically runs **1.8%–2.1%** effective rate — lower than Leander MUD communities and competitive with Georgetown.

On a $380K home:
- Liberty Hill at 1.9%: **$7,220/year** ($602/month)
- Leander MUD at 2.4%: **$9,120/year** ($760/month)

That's $158/month or roughly $1,900/year in savings — real money over a 5–7 year hold.

---

## The Commute Reality

This is the key trade-off. Liberty Hill sits off US-183A but further north/west than Leander.

**Liberty Hill to Austin tech hubs:**
- Domain / North Austin: 45–65 minutes depending on traffic
- Downtown Austin: 55–75 minutes
- Tech Ridge: 50–65 minutes
- Fort Cavazos: ~55 minutes

**vs. Leander:**
- Domain / North Austin: 30–45 minutes
- Downtown Austin: 45–60 minutes

The extra 15–20 minutes each way adds up. For a 5-day commuter, that's 2.5–3.5 extra hours per week. If you're hybrid (3 days/week), it's more manageable.

**Remote workers** looking for the best value for their dollar should seriously consider Liberty Hill — the commute penalty disappears for fully remote households.

---

## Liberty Hill ISD vs. Leander ISD

This is the main reason Liberty Hill stays cheaper than Leander.

**Liberty Hill ISD:** Growing rapidly, new facilities being built, improving rankings. Not in the same tier as LISD yet. Graduates go to college at strong rates but the AP program and extracurricular depth aren't at Vista Ridge levels.

**Leander ISD:** Consistently #2 in the metro, three strong high schools, established programs.

For buyers with elementary-age children who plan to stay 10+ years, the LISD premium might pay off in ways that Liberty Hill doesn't match yet. For buyers without school-age kids, Liberty Hill is compelling.

---

## Who Should Buy in Liberty Hill

**Best fit:**
- Remote workers or hybrid (2–3 days/week) who want maximum space and value
- Buyers priced out of Leander who still want new construction and a master-planned community
- Buyers who want hill country character and lot size over urban proximity
- Buyers who don't have school-age children or are considering private school regardless

**Less ideal:**
- Daily commuters to Austin who value time strongly
- Families with middle or high school kids who specifically want LISD programs
- Buyers who want walkability or proximity to dining/retail
