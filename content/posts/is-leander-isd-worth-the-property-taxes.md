# Is Leander ISD Worth the Property Taxes? An Honest Answer for 2026 Buyers

The number one question I get from buyers comparing Leander to other Austin suburbs is some version of this: **"The property taxes in Leander are higher — is the school district actually worth paying extra for?"**

It's a legitimate financial question. Here's the honest answer.

---

## First, What Are the Actual Numbers?

**Leander's effective property tax rate: 2.1%–2.5%** (varies by community — MUD district homes run higher)

On a $450K home, that's **$9,450–$11,250/year** — or $787–$937/month added to your mortgage.

**For comparison:**
- **Round Rock ISD areas:** ~1.9%–2.1% effective rate
- **Georgetown ISD areas:** ~1.8%–2.2% effective rate
- **Cedar Park areas (Leander ISD):** ~1.9%–2.2% effective rate (often lower than central Leander due to no MUD)
- **Liberty Hill ISD:** ~1.8%–2.0% effective rate

On a $450K home, the difference between Leander and Round Rock ISD areas is roughly **$900–$2,700/year** in additional property taxes. That's real money.

---

## What Does Leander ISD Actually Deliver?

**The rankings:**
- **#2 school district in the Austin metro** (Niche, 2025)
- **Top 15% of school districts in Texas** overall
- All three high schools (Leander, Vista Ridge, Rouse) made the AP School Honor Roll in 2025
- A-rated overall from Niche based on academics, teachers, college prep, and clubs/activities

**What that looks like on the ground:**
- Strong AP program participation — Vista Ridge alone runs 30+ AP courses
- Competitive UIL programs across academic and athletic categories
- Above-average counselor-to-student ratios compared to comparable Texas districts
- Lower student-to-teacher ratios than RRISD at most campuses

**The honest caveat:** Not every campus in LISD is equal. The district manages significant enrollment growth, which means some newer schools are still developing extracurricular depth. Your specific campus assignment (determined by your home address) matters — ask to verify it before closing.

---

## Is Leander ISD Meaningfully Better Than Round Rock ISD?

This is the real comparison most buyers are making.

**The data:** Both districts are rated A by Niche. Round Rock ISD is the larger, older district — RRISD graduates have gone to UT, A&M, and ivy-leagues for decades. It's an excellent school district by any measure.

**Where Leander ISD edges out RRISD:**
- Newer, more recently built school facilities (Leander's growth funded new campuses)
- Slightly higher AP participation rates and college-going rates in recent cohorts
- Smaller district size means less bureaucratic inertia

**Where RRISD is competitive or better:**
- More established high school programs in fine arts and some athletics
- Some individual campuses within RRISD outperform comparable LISD campuses
- Lower property tax rate in many Round Rock ISD areas

**Bottom line:** If you're agonizing over Leander ISD vs. Round Rock ISD specifically, you're comparing two strong districts and the school difference alone probably doesn't justify the tax premium. The neighborhood, commute, and home value are likely bigger differentiators for your decision.

---

## The Real Reason the Tax Premium Is Worth It for Most Families

Here's what the data actually shows: **Leander ISD home values hold better over time than comparable homes in lower-rated districts.**

When you pay more in property taxes for a top-tier school district, you're also buying into a demand floor on your home's value. Buyers consistently pay a premium to be in LISD — which means when you sell, your resale pool is larger and better-qualified.

On a $450K home held for 7 years:
- If Leander ISD commands a 5% price premium vs. a comparable non-LISD home — that's $22,500 in additional resale value
- The annual tax premium is $900–$2,700/year — or $6,300–$18,900 over 7 years

In the best-case scenario the math works out roughly equal. In the typical scenario, the district premium in sale price roughly offsets the higher taxes over a normal hold period. You're not losing money by being in LISD — but you're not banking extra money from the district alone either.

**Where you do gain:** Quality of life, school community, and the practical reality that your kids are in a well-funded, well-run district with options.

---

## Who Should Factor the Tax Premium Most Heavily?

**It matters most if you:**
- Plan to stay under 5 years (you won't recoup the premium in appreciation)
- Don't have school-age children and aren't planning to
- Are comparing a Leander home in a MUD district to a Cedar Park home in LISD (same district, potentially lower taxes)

**It matters less if you:**
- Have kids who will attend LISD for 6–12+ years
- Plan to hold the home long-term (10+ years)
- Are comparing to non-LISD districts where the quality gap is more significant

---

## The Underrated Option: Cedar Park in LISD

If you want Leander ISD schools with a lower tax rate, consider Cedar Park homes that are assigned to LISD campuses. The Leander ISD boundary extends into significant portions of Cedar Park. In those areas, you get LISD schools without the Leander MUD tax issues that drive rates up.

Ask your agent specifically to filter by LISD boundary and compare effective tax rates by address — this can save $1,000–$2,000/year with identical school assignments.

---

## The Short Answer

**Yes, Leander ISD is worth paying for** — if you have school-age children, plan to stay 7+ years, and understand that the premium is real but offset by strong resale demand.

**No, it's not worth the premium** — if you're childless, planning a short hold, or comparing to Cedar Park addresses that are also in LISD at lower tax rates.
