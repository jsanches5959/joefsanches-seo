# Best New Construction Homes in Cedar Park TX 2026: Builders, Communities & Prices

Cedar Park is one of the most underrated new construction markets in the Austin area. While Leander gets more attention, several Cedar Park communities offer competitive pricing, Leander ISD school assignments, and shorter commutes to the 183A tech corridor — without the MUD tax premium that inflates tax bills in newer Leander developments.

Here's what's available in Cedar Park new construction in 2026.

---

## Why Cedar Park Instead of Leander?

The Cedar Park vs. Leander decision usually comes down to three things:

**Property taxes:** Cedar Park addresses in established areas often carry lower effective tax rates (1.9%–2.2%) than newer Leander communities in MUD districts (2.2%–2.5%). On a $450K home, that can be $1,000–$2,000/year in savings with identical school assignments.

**Commute:** Cedar Park sits closer to the 183A toll road and the Lakeline Metro Rail station. Tech workers commuting to North Austin or the Domain often prefer Cedar Park's positioning.

**School zone:** A significant portion of Cedar Park is assigned to Leander ISD campuses — not Cedar Park or Round Rock ISD. If LISD is your priority, verify the specific address. Many buyers don't know they can get LISD schools from a Cedar Park address at a lower tax rate.

**Notes:** One of the most talked-about new communities in the area. Planned walkable town center with retail and restaurants. Mix of Cedar Park and Leander addresses depending on the phase. Strong resale demand expected as retail build-out completes.

**Builder:** David Weekley, Ashton Woods  
**Price range:** $420K–$580K

**Builder:** Various builders  
**Price range:** $350K–$500K  
**Schools:** Leander ISD (most addresses)

**Notes:** Golf course community, highly desirable. New construction is limited — primarily resale.

## Builder Incentives in Cedar Park: June 2026

Most Cedar Park builders are matching Leander incentive packages in 2026:

## Cedar Park vs. Leander: The Tax Math (2026)

On a $450,000 home:

| Area | Effective Rate | Annual Tax | Monthly |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cedar Park (established) | 1.95% | $8,775 | $731 |
| Cedar Park (newer MUD) | 2.15% | $9,675 | $806 |
| Leander (non-MUD) | 2.1% | $9,450 | $788 |
| Leander (MUD district) | 2.45% | $11,025 | $919 |

The difference between best-case Cedar Park and worst-case Leander MUD: **$188/month on the same home value**. That compounds over a 7-year hold to approximately $15,800.

The catch: newer Cedar Park communities are also adding MUD districts. Always verify the specific address before assuming a Cedar Park address = lower taxes.

## Leander ISD in Cedar Park: Which Campuses?

Cedar Park addresses assigned to LISD include a mix of elementary, middle, and high school assignments. The main LISD high schools serving Cedar Park addresses:

- **Rouse High School** — commonly assigned to central Cedar Park LISD addresses
- **Leander High School** — some western Cedar Park addresses
- **Vista Ridge** — primarily Leander proper but some Cedar Park fringe addresses

All three Leander ISD high schools made the AP School Honor Roll in 2025. The campus you're assigned to matters less than the district you're in — all three are well-resourced.
